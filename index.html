I am a post-doc at Stanford University working with Percy Liang. Starting Fall 2019, I will be joining NYU CS as an assistant professor. Prior to Stanford, I got my Ph.D. in Computer Science from University of Maryland, College Park, advised by Hal Daumé III and Jordan Boyd-Graber. I am broadly interested in machine learning and natural language processing.

I want to enable machines to communicate with people in a natural, efficient way. Towards this goal, my research focuses on language understanding in an interactive environment. Recent directions include dialogue systems and controllable text generation.

Prospective students: I am looking for students to join my group next Fall. Please apply to either the PhD program in Computer Science or PhD program in Data Science.
